A drop in China's imports could drag these countries down

In the year to July, China's customs agency reports that imports from Australia are down by $15bn dollars on the same period last year - a loss which is already equal to 1% of Australia's GDP. China's imports overall are down by 14.6% over 2015. Find out what happens if this decline continues for the rest of the year, and how that loss compares to each country's GDP

Thursday 13 August 2015

Asia

Around the world

The effects of a slowdown in China are felt all over the world; China's near neighbours are tied in to its manufacturing processes. Other countries supply it with oil and gas, while some far-away places in Africa and South America provide it with metals and other primary materials. Agricultural produce is important to Brazil and New Zealand. Lastly, several European countries are surprisingly tied in, often for luxury goods.
